Jeans products:

Jeans differ from each other not only in cut, design and size, but also in denim density (or, as they sometimes say, denim weight). This density is measured in ounces per square or linear yard (oz./yd2, oz./yd. Or, as they write much more often, just oz.). It is customary to highlight lightweight denim, mid-weight denim and heavyweight denim. Later in this article, we will go into detail about all three varieties.

  • Mid-weight denim - 12 to 15 ounces (oz).
  • Lightweight denim - 5 to 11 ounces (oz.)
  • Heavyweight denim - from 16 ounces (oz).

    linens:

    Bed linen is made from low-pile cotton flannel. This fabric is softer than any other, and its strength is comparable to calico. The flannel bed can be washed and ironed at any temperature, it does not form pellets for a long time. Flannel retains heat better than any other fabric - it is not for nothing that it is called winter cotton, it will be hot under such linen in summer. If you use flannel bedding only in cold weather, it will last for a long time.

    Satin:

    In high-density satin, the threads are very strongly twisted, so the fabric is smooth and slightly shiny. The paint on satin adheres very well, new linen will not fade and will not fade over time. A set made of thick satin will last longer than any other cotton linen. Due to the diagonal intersection of the threads, it hardly wrinkles, but in terms of smoothness and softness it is inferior to satin fabrics.

    Towels:

    Terry cloth is created from cotton threads, which, in turn, are spun from many cotton fibers. The longer these fibers are, the stronger the thread will be, and, accordingly, the product. The length of the fibers that make up the cotton thread also affects the texture of the fabric obtained: the longer they are, the softer and fluffier the terry product will turn out, the better the product will absorb water. Although the absorbent quality of terry is its hygroscopicity, the composition of the fiber also affects the absorbent quality. The soft terry cloth absorbs moisture well and dries quickly.
